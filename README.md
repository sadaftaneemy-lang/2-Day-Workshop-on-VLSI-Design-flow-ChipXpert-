## What is Conductor ?
A conductor is a material that allows electric current (flow of electrons) to pass through it easily because it has a large number of free electrons.

## What is Semiconductor ??
A semiconductor is a material whose ability to conduct electricity is between a conductor and an insulator.It can act as a conductor or an insulator depending on conditions like temperature, voltage, or doping

## What is Insulator ??
An insulator is a material that does NOT allow electric current to flow easily because it has very few or no free electrons

# Real worlds Chip usage

Very-Large-Scale Integration (VLSI) technology forms the backbone of modern electronic systems by enabling the integration of millions to billions of transistors onto a single semiconductor chip. These VLSI chips are widely used in real-world applications such as smartphones, laptops, electric vehicles, smartwatches, smart televisions, and medical devices, where high performance and low power consumption are critical. In mobile computing, advanced system-on-chip (SoC) designs are employed to integrate processors, memory interfaces, graphics units, and communication modules on a single chip, allowing efficient operation of smartphones and tablets. Similarly, laptops and desktop computers rely on high-performance processors designed using advanced VLSI techniques to deliver fast computation, multitasking, and energy efficiency.

In smart and embedded systems, including Internet of Things (IoT) devices, wearables, and smart home appliances, custom VLSI chips are designed with a strong emphasis on low power operation, compact size, and reliability. These chips support sensing, data processing, wireless connectivity, and basic artificial intelligence functions, enabling intelligent decision-making at the device level. Furthermore, continuous scaling in VLSI technology has made it possible to fabricate chips containing tens of billions of transistors within an extremely small silicon area, often smaller than a human fingernail. This high level of integration improves performance, reduces cost per function, and enhances functionality, making VLSI an essential technology for modern digital and analog electronic systems.
